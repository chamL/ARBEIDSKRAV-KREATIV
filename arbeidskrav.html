The Ali-Liston
Rivalry
Two Epic Showdowns

In the 1960s, the boxing world was treated to two epic clashes between Muhammad Ali and Sonny Liston. These historic bouts, in 1964 and 1965, remain among the most iconic moments in sports history. The first fight, in 1964, saw a young and confident Cassius Clay (later known as Muhammad Ali) facing the reigning heavyweight champion, Sonny Liston. Ali's victory in this bout marked the beginning of his journey to greatness.

Their rematch in 1965, featuring the famous "Phantom Punch," left an indelible mark on boxing lore, adding to the mystique of their rivalry. These two fights between Ali and Liston are remembered not only for their intensity but also for their significance in the career of the legendary Muhammad Ali.

The Dream Continues

The battles against Sonny Liston were just the beginning. Muhammad Ali's dream of boxing greatness carried him through a series of iconic showdowns. These top fights were pivotal moments where Ali's dream met reality. In the "Fight of the Century" against Joe Frazier in 1971, Ali faced his first pro defeat but emerged stronger, reaffirming his dream. The historic "Thrilla in Manila" rematch showcased his unwavering spirit and a dream that refused to be extinguished. Ali's dream extended beyond the ring, exemplified by his "Rumble in the Jungle" with George Foreman. This fight was more than just a victory it was a manifestation of his dream's power. Through legendary bouts against Ken Norton and Leon Spinks, Ali's dream remained undiminished.
